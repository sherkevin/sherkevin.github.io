About Me

I am a research-oriented LLM application algorithm candidate with a focus on calibration and reliability: whether a multimodal model is visually grounded, whether an embedding score reflects substitutability, whether a recommender should keep asking, and whether a domain RAG system can answer with evidence and safety.

My work spans multimodal recommendation at MiniMax, Tool Use and Agent infrastructure at ByteDance, and domain LLM/RAG diagnosis for lithography systems at SMEE. I am currently seeking full-time LLM application algorithm roles with both engineering impact and research depth.

Selected Research

ACM MM, under review · independent first author

CHORD: Calibrating Hallucinations via Object-Resonant Decoding in MLLMs

Object-grounded decoding for hallucination calibration. The work studies how query-relevant visual anchors, late-layer visual attention, and short-horizon rollout can verify whether a generation path remains visually grounded.

Manuscript withheld during double-blind review. Public summary only.

RecSys, under review · independent first author

Does Similarity Imply Substitutability? Typed Score Corrections for Frozen Alignment

Studies relation collapse in frozen text-to-CF alignment. The project asks when semantic similarity fails to mean behavioral substitutability and uses typed post-scoring correction to calibrate recommendation candidates.

RecSys, under review · independent first author

When to Stop Asking? A Top-K-Aware Stopping Layer for Conversational Recommendation

A calibrated stopping mechanism for conversational recommendation. Instead of relying on top-1 confidence, the method watches Top-K boundary stability to balance recommendation success and user burden.
