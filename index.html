Selected research

Calibration is the common thread.

MLLM · under review

CHORD: Calibrating Hallucinations via Object-Resonant Decoding

A decoding-time approach for object hallucination calibration: query-relevant visual anchors, late-layer visual attention, and short-horizon rollout are combined to verify whether a candidate generation path remains visually grounded.

Manuscript withheld during double-blind review. Public summary only.

RecSys · under review

Does Similarity Imply Substitutability?

Studies relation collapse in frozen text-to-CF alignment. The key idea is that semantic similarity is not the same as behavioral substitutability, so typed post-scoring correction can recover recommendation roles without retraining the backbone.

Independent first author; public details limited during review.

LLM4Rec · under review

When to Stop Asking?

A Top-K-aware stopping layer for conversational recommendation. Instead of relying on top-1 confidence, the method watches the stability of the Top-K boundary and balances recommendation success with user burden.

Independent first author; public details limited during review.
